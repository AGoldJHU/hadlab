Website Redesign

Executive Summary for Review

February 2026  •  Prepared by Drew

What We Built

A complete set of HTML page mockups for your new website, designed around a magazine-style editorial aesthetic. The design leads with your ideas and positioning—not just credentials—to signal thought leadership across academic, policy, and public audiences.

Typography

Playfair Display headlines + Source Sans Pro body (Georgia/sans-serif fallbacks) — authoritative yet readable

Colors

Dark teal (#234e52) + copper accent (#c77d3c)

Navigation

Sticky top bar: Home | Research | Publications | Talks | Media | Normativity Lab | About

Layout

Information-dense, editorially restrained — no animations, gradients, or stock photos

Pages Ready for Review

Homepage (6 variants)

Recommended: Magazine editorial style — dramatic hero text (“Building AI That Understands Human Systems”), “Current Foci” feature section on new paradigms for AI development and agile governance, two-column publications + research themes grid, media coverage strip, and contact panel.

The key design decision: Your ideas lead, credentials follow. Instead of starting with headshot + CV, the hero declares what you do. This positions you as a thought leader, not just a professor.

Alternative variants available:

•       Navy/gold colors — traditional academic gravitas over contemporary positioning

•       Headshot-prominent (2 versions) — personal brand emphasis for media/speaking

•       Foreground variant — different hero visual treatment, same structure

•       Current site style — minimal visual change, improved information architecture

Research Page

Three numbered themes organized around research questions rather than findings. Each includes driving questions and featured papers. Themes are ordered by priority: Normative AI, Multi-Agent Systems, AI Governance.

Publications Page

Curated selection organized by research theme with venue badges (Science, Nature, PNAS) and placed in chronological order. Deliberately not comprehensive—signals editorial judgment. Jump links at top for quick navigation.

Talks Page

Card-based layout showcasing selected video talks. Each card includes a video icon, talk title, event and date metadata, description, and direct link to watch. Featured talks represent a range of venues and topics. Links to full YouTube playlist for comprehensive collection.

Bio Page

Narrative focus. Frontloads current research focus, then follows standard academic bio structure. Features a photo of the evolutionary workshop. CV download at the end.

Normativity Lab Page

Full team roster with embedded headshots, organized by role (PI, Research Scientists, Doctoral Students, Undergrads, Staff). Within each role the members are alphabetized by first name. Includes links to their personal websites or social media sites.

Media Page

Section-based layout with sticky section navigation (Podcasts & Interviews, Press Coverage). Organized chronologically within sections with year dividers. Showcases press coverage, podcasts, and speaking engagements. Reinforces public intellectual positioning.

Policy Page

Mostly the same as current websites with some updates from Muhamed. Highlights testimony and policy engagement with core policy focus areas (Regulatory Markets, AI Registration & Transparency, International Coordination). Includes selected policy publications. Reinforces public intellectual positioning.

Decisions Needed From You

1.     Which homepage variant? The recommended editorial style leads with ideas. The headshot variants lead with personal brand. Navy/gold is more traditionally academic. Review all six and pick a direction.

2.     Research theme labels — Are the three themes (Normative AI, Multi-Agent Systems, AI Governance) the right framing? These structure the Research and Publications pages.

3.     Content accuracy — Spot-check publication details, dates, co-authors, and team member bios for anything that needs correcting.

The Design Philosophy in 30 Seconds

The site is designed to position you as a public intellectual and policy-engaged researcher, not just an academic with a CV online.

How to Review the Files

All files are self-contained HTML—just open them in any browser. No special software needed. They’re in your Dropbox and organized by page type.

Start with the recommended homepage (Homepage_1_V5.html), then look at the Research and Publications pages to see how they connect. The other homepage variants are there for comparison if you want a different direction.
